📈 14-Day Trend — Productivity · Speed · Accuracy

Productivity = Active Minutes ÷ 390 (standard daily working minutes) × 100%. Speed % = Target Minutes ÷ Rendered Minutes × 100, capped at 150%. Accuracy is volume-weighted across a person's graded roles for the day (Picker graded downstream by Staging Checker discrepancies; Staging/Outbound Checker graded on their own logged discrepancies; Puller is untracked — shown as "—"). Every personnel on file always holds a rank on whichever board matches their role(s), even with zero logged activity for the selected Activity Day — ranking within a board is by a single selected metric only, with zero-activity personnel always placed last. A person holding roles in both pools (uncommon) appears on both boards, scored only on that board's roles.

🧾 Checking Operations Rankings (Staging Checker + Outbound Checker)

#PersonnelShipmentsQty Hit/MissSpeed % Incident Miss %Idle %Idle CauseProductivityAccuracy

Hit = Actual Rendered Time ≤ Target Time for that shipment group; Miss otherwise. Speed % = Target Minutes ÷ Rendered Minutes × 100, averaged across every shipment-group task for that person and capped at 150% (e.g. one task at 100% and a second at 80% averages to 90%) — 100% = exactly on target, above 100% = faster than target. Productivity % and Idle % are both computed from the person's FULL COMBINED ACTIVITY across every role/pool they work, not just this board's role group — so a Picker who also Checks has both counted together. Productivity % is computed per Activity Day (that day's active minutes across all roles ÷ 6.5 standard working hours — uncapped, so an overtime day can push it past 100%) then averaged across every day they had recorded activity over the selected Report Period. Idle % = total idle minutes (gaps between back-to-back tasks in ANY role, same-day only) ÷ (6.5hrs × number of days with recorded activity) — e.g. 10 active days = idle minutes ÷ (6.5×60×10). Idle Cause explains WHY, not just when: each idle gap is checked against (a) whether ≥70% of this board's active personnel were simultaneously idle (zero task in progress) at that moment, and (b) how many distinct shipments were completed system-wide (every role) during that gap versus a rate-scaled threshold (<5/hr of gap length) — No Dispatches = team-wide idle + thin dispatch volume (nothing to hand out), Early Completion = team-wide idle but volume wasn't actually low (backlog ran out faster than expected), Individual = idle while most teammates were still working (not explained by lack of work). A person's row shows whichever cause covers the majority of their idle minutes that day, or Mixed if no cause clearly leads. A person holding both roles in a group (e.g. Picker + Puller) has tasks from both merged into one row. Picker Accuracy is graded downstream from Staging Checker discrepancies on the same shipment; Staging/Outbound Checker Accuracy is graded from their own logged discrepancies; Puller Accuracy is not separately tracked (—). Every personnel on file who holds a role in a board's pool always appears on that board, even with zero logged activity for the selected Activity Day — zero-activity personnel show dashes and always sink to the bottom regardless of sort.

👤 Personnel Roster

Header row required: Personnel, Main Role (Secondary Role / Third Role / Fourth Role columns are optional). Each role value must exactly match: Picker, Staging Checker, Puller, Outbound Checker. Legacy "Designation" header is still accepted as Main Role.

Main / Secondary / Third / Fourth Role define every role a person may be assigned to (there are only 4 possible roles, so this covers every combination). Each role still gets its own activity logs and target — the Role Leaderboards group Picker + Puller into one "Picking Operations" board and Staging Checker + Outbound Checker into one "Checking Operations" board, so a person's work under any of their roles counts on the right board.

📍 Staging Area Masterlist

Header row required: Staging Area — one area name per line after that.

This is the masterlist a Staging Checker picks from when confirming a check — at least one area is required per completion.

⏱️ Break Window Scheduler

Duration is derived automatically from start/end. Break windows are subtracted from worked time whenever a task's active period overlaps them.

🕕 Duty Shift Schedule

Fixed shifts set by the admin — e.g. AM = 6:00 AM–3:00 PM, PM = 3:00 PM–12:00 AM. Reporting for duty anywhere inside a shift's window locks that person's schedule to the shift's official start/end (e.g. tapping in at 7:40 AM under AM still records 6:00 AM–3:00 PM). Break time is separate — see Break Windows above; it's checked automatically against whichever shift the person is on.
